# ee201c: basic-electrical-and-electronics-engineering
  - (EE201C: Basic Electrical and Electronics Engineering)

## Course Objectives
* To introduce fundamental concepts of electrical circuits and machines.  
* To provide a basic understanding of semiconductor devices and electronic circuits.  
* To explain the working principles of electrical machines and their aerospace applications.  
* To familiarize students with measurement systems and instrumentation basics.  

## Course Content

### Module 1: DC Circuits
* Basic electrical quantities – current, voltage, power, and energy.  
* Ohm’s law, Kirchhoff’s laws.  
* Series and parallel circuits.  
* Mesh and nodal analysis.  
* Superposition, Thevenin’s, and Norton’s theorems.  

### Module 2: AC Circuits
* Generation of alternating voltages and currents.  
* RMS and average values, form factor, power factor.  
* Phasor representation and impedance concept.  
* Resonance in RLC circuits.  
* Three-phase systems – star and delta connections, power measurement.  

### Module 3: Electrical Machines
* Working principle and construction of DC machines.  
* Transformers – EMF equation, efficiency, and testing.  
* Three-phase induction motors – torque–speed characteristics.  
* Alternators and synchronous motors – basic operation.  
* Applications in aircraft electrical systems.  

### Module 4: Semiconductor Devices
* Semiconductor materials – intrinsic and extrinsic types.  
* PN junction diode – characteristics and applications.  
* Zener diode, BJT, FET – principle and configurations.  
* Rectifiers, voltage regulators, and amplifiers.  
* Introduction to op-amps and basic applications.  

### Module 5: Measurements and Instrumentation
* Construction and working of ammeters, voltmeters, wattmeters, and energy meters.  
* Measurement of resistance, inductance, and capacitance.  
* Digital multimeters and oscilloscopes.  
* Transducers and sensors – temperature, pressure, displacement.  
* Instrumentation in aerospace systems.
