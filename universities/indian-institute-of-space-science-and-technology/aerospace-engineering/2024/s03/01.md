# ma211c: complex-analysis-and-pde
  - (MA211C: Complex Analysis and PDE)

## Course Objectives
* To provide fundamental understanding of complex numbers and their properties
* To study complex functions, limits, continuity and differentiation
* To understand Cauchy-Riemann equations and analytic functions
* To learn contour integrals and Cauchy's integral theorems
* To study power series, Taylor series, and Laurent series
* To understand partial differential equations and their classification
* To solve initial and boundary value problems using separation of variables

## Course Content

### Module 1: Complex Analysis Fundamentals
* Complex numbers and elementary properties
* Complex functions - limits, continuity and differentiation
* Cauchy-Riemann equations
* Analytic and harmonic functions
* Elementary analytic functions

### Module 2: Complex Integration
* Anti-derivatives and line (contour) integrals
* Cauchy-Goursat theorem
* Cauchy's integral formula
* Morera's theorem and Liouville's theorem

### Module 3: Series and Residues
* Power series and Taylor series
* Zeros of analytic functions
* Singularities and Laurent series
* Residues and Cauchy's Residue theorem
* Applications of Cauchy's Residue theorem to evaluate improper integrals

### Module 4: Transformations and First Order PDEs
* Bilinear Transformations and Conformal Mapping
* Introduction to PDEs and modelling
* First order partial differential equations
* Solutions of linear and quasilinear first order PDEs
* Method of characteristics and transport equation

### Module 5: Second Order PDEs and Applications
* Shocks and rarefactions waves, weak solution
* Charpit method
* Classification of second-order PDEs and canonical form
* Solution of Initial and boundary value problems involving Laplace, heat and wave equations
* Method of separation of variables and Fourier series
