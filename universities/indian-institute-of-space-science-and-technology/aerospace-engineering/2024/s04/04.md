# ae206c: aerospace-vehicle-propulsion
  - (AE206C: Aerospace Vehicle Propulsion)

## Course Objectives
* To introduce the fundamental principles and thermodynamic analysis of aerospace propulsion systems.  
* To study the performance characteristics of air-breathing engines.  
* To understand the operating principles of rockets and propulsion system components.  
* To analyse propulsion cycles relevant to aircraft and space vehicle applications.  

## Course Content

### Module 1: Introduction to Propulsion Systems
* Classification of propulsion systems – air-breathing and non-air-breathing.  
* Propulsive efficiency and overall efficiency.  
* Thrust generation and thrust equation.  
* Specific impulse, specific fuel consumption, and performance parameters.  
* Comparison of jet and rocket propulsion.  

### Module 2: Thermodynamics of Air-Breathing Engines
* Ideal gas turbine cycle and Brayton cycle analysis.  
* Efficiencies – compressor, turbine, and overall cycle efficiency.  
* Effect of pressure ratio and temperature ratio on performance.  
* Real-cycle analysis and losses.  
* Application to turbojet and turbofan engines.  

### Module 3: Components of Jet Engines
* Construction and working of compressors (axial and centrifugal).  
* Combustion chamber types and performance parameters.  
* Turbines – axial flow design and power extraction.  
* Nozzles – convergent and convergent-divergent types.  
* Intake and exhaust systems – design considerations.  

### Module 4: Rocket Propulsion
* Types of rocket engines – solid, liquid, and hybrid.  
* Thrust chamber, injector, and nozzle design.  
* Rocket equation and performance parameters.  
* Propellants – characteristics and selection.  
* Staging and trajectory considerations.  

### Module 5: Advanced Propulsion Concepts
* Ramjet, scramjet, and pulsejet engines – principles and applications.  
* Electric propulsion – ion, plasma, and Hall thrusters.  
* Nuclear and solar propulsion (overview).  
* Environmental and efficiency considerations in propulsion.  
* Trends in future aerospace propulsion systems.
