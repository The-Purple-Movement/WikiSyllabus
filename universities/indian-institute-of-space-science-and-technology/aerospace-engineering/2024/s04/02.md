# ae221c: compressible-flow
  - (AE221C: Compressible Flow)

## Course Objectives
* To understand governing equations for compressible flows
* To study wave propagation and shock phenomena
* To analyze normal and oblique shock waves
* To examine flows with friction and heat transfer
* To learn about transonic flows and linearized potential flow

## Course Content

### Module 1: Fundamental Equations and Waves
* Governing equations for compressible flows
* Quasi-one-dimensional flow analysis
* Acoustic waves and waves of finite amplitude
* Wave propagation characteristics
* Basic compressible flow concepts

### Module 2: Shock Waves and Applications
* Normal shocks and Rankine-Hugoniot equations
* Shock tube problem analysis
* Oblique shocks and their properties
* Prandtl-Meyer expansion waves
* Wave drag calculations

### Module 3: Wave Interactions and Conical Flows
* Reflection and interaction of waves
* Conical flow analysis
* Complex wave pattern interactions
* Applications in nozzle and inlet design
* Supersonic flow configurations

### Module 4: Flow with Transport Effects
* Flows with friction effects (Fanno flow)
* Flows with heat transfer (Rayleigh flow)
* Combined transport phenomena
* Thermodynamic considerations
* Practical applications in propulsion

### Module 5: Advanced Compressible Flows
* Linearized potential flow theory
* Applications of linearized theory
* Transonic flow characteristics
* Critical Mach number concepts
* Compressibility effects on aircraft performance
