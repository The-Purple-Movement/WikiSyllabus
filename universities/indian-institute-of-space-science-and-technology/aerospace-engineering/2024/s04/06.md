# hs221h: social-sciences-and-engineering-ethics
  - (HS221H: Social Sciences and Engineering Ethics)

## Course Objectives
* To understand social science perspectives and theoretical frameworks
* To learn methodology for studying society and social problems
* To examine social structures and cultural aspects
* To analyze problems of marginalized communities
* To understand engineering ethics and professional responsibilities

## Course Content

### Module 1: Social Science Foundations
* Social Science Perspective and Characteristics
* Common Sense Knowledge vs Social Science Knowledge
* Emergence and development of Social Science
* Theoretical Perspectives: Functionalism, Structuralism, Conflict, Post Modern
* Philosophy of Science and Social Science

### Module 2: Research Methodology and Social Structure
* Statement of the Problem in social research
* Literature Review techniques
* Universe, Unit and Sampling methods
* Qualitative and Quantitative Research Methods
* Social Structure and Types of Societies

### Module 3: Social Institutions and Culture
* Family, Kinship and Marriage systems and changes
* Culture and Socialization processes
* Agencies of Socialization
* Race, Ethnicity and Nation States concepts
* Caste and Tribe in social structure

### Module 4: Governance and Social Issues
* Transparency, Civil Society and Good governance
* Feminivities, Masculinities and Gender relations
* Sexuality and Gender issues
* Tribal Society and Development Induced Displacement
* Poverty and social inequality

### Module 5: Marginalized Groups and Engineering Ethics
* Women in India: Sex Ratio and Violence issues
* Children: Foeticide, Infanticide, Child marriage and labour
* Elderly in India and People with Disabilities
* Sexual Minorities and their challenges
* Introduction to Engineering Ethics and Professional ethics
